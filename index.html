Healing together, one horse at a time.

Equiatude Foundation pairs women and children healing from abuse with rehabilitated off-track thoroughbreds — horses retired from racing, given a new purpose: helping survivors rebuild trust, confidence, and connection.

Why Rescued Horses Rescue Women

Two journeys, one healing.

Abuse can shatter a person's sense of self-worth. Survivors often carry the weight of PTSD — struggling to trust, to feel safe, to believe in themselves again. Horses coming off the racetrack carry their own scars too: physical injuries, emotional guardedness, a need for patience before they can trust their new role.

When the two come together, something remarkable happens. Healing stops being something done to someone, and becomes something built together.

Goal-Oriented Programs

Every session is built around what a client needs — quieting the noise of the mind and rebuilding a sense of purpose, step by step.

A Peaceful Place

Our farm is designed for safety and reflection — space to breathe deeply, connect with nature, and begin a journey of self-discovery.

Rescued, Rehabilitated

Every horse in our program is an off-track thoroughbred given the time, patience, and care to become a steady, trusted partner.
